ConWiki: Help

Find An Article

For searches, there is a search box at the top of every page. Type what you are looking for in the box and ConWiki will try it's best to match your query. Select any page in the list to go to that page. There are other ways to browse and explore ConWiki articles; many of these will be links in one article leading to another.

Edit An Article

To edit an article click the edit button in the bar at the top of any page. Once you click on that button you will be able to change the contents of the article. You will also be able to add style to the article with ConWiki's styling syntax.

Report A Problem

If during your time on ConWiki you encounter any problems, you can report them by visiting this GitHub page. Once at the site you can open a issue, and I will try to fix it as soon as possible. However, you can also try to fix these problems your-self by contributing to the code that makes up ConWiki on the same GitHub page. Even suggestions or ideas for how to improve ConWiki are always welcome.

Create An Article

To create an article click the create button in the bar at the top of any page. Once you click on that button ConWiki will take you to an article creation page. Then an empty article will be created, after that you are free to edit the article as much as you want. Each article must contain a name to save and load the article later.

Style An Article

To add style to your article such as: links, images, headings etc. ConWiki has specific keywords to add these. If you already know how styling works in wikipedia then most of the syntax will remain the same. For more help, please visit the GitHub page for more info.

Navigation On ConWiki

On every single ConWiki page, there will be a side bar with links to many different parts of ConWiki, your articles and other websites. The side bar is split into 2 main sections: ConWiki, Articles.
ConWiki navigation, contains links to all different parts of ConWiki such as this page and home.
Articles, contains files for all of your articles. These files might be for: languages, cultures, geography, world or anything you can think of.
